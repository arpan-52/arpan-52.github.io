Arpan Pal

Radio Astronomer

Fulbright-Nehru Doctoral Fellow at NRAO, Socorro

I study cosmic magnetism and astrophysical shocks in merging galaxy clusters using radio observations. My research combines radio polarimetry, interferometry, and data analysis to understand the fundamental physics of the Universe.

Research

Radio Relics and Polarization in Galaxy Cluster Mergers

Investigating linearly polarized radio relics down to 550 MHz using uGMRT and MeerKAT. Understanding magnetic field amplification in merger-driven shocks.

2024-2025

Fast Radio Burst Periodicity Studies

Analyzing activity periodicity in FRB 20240209A and polarization properties. Collaboration with international research teams.

2024-2025

Radio Interferometric Data Processing

Developing custom pipelines: CHARIZARD (full polarimetric imaging), catboss (RFI flagging), and Foresight (sky model estimation).

2021-Present

Observational Expertise

PI on 250 hours of uGMRT time, 93.5 hours of VLA time, and 64 ks of XMM-Newton observations. Co-PI on additional observing proposals.
