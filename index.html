About Me

I am Arpan Pal, a radio astronomer currently working as a Fulbright-Nehru Doctoral Fellow at the National Radio Astronomy Observatory in Socorro, New Mexico. I study cosmic magnetism and astrophysical shocks in merging galaxy clusters using advanced radio observations and interferometry.

Current Work

My research combines radio polarimetry, interferometric imaging, and computational data analysis. I focus on understanding magnetic field evolution in merger-driven shocks and analyzing fast radio burst properties using cutting-edge radio facilities.

Research Interests

Cosmic magnetism, Radio polarimetry, Galaxy cluster mergers, Astrophysical shocks, Radio interferometry, Fast radio bursts, Radio imaging

Research

Radio Relics and Polarization in Merging Clusters

Investigating linearly polarized radio relics down to 550 MHz using uGMRT and MeerKAT. Understanding the origin and evolution of magnetic fields in merger-driven shocks.

Fast Radio Burst Periodicity

Analyzing four-month periodicity in FRB 20240209A activity and polarization properties. Collaboration on pulsar-like polarization position angle swings in nearby FRBs published in Nature.

Radio Interferometric Data Processing

Developing custom pipelines including CHARIZARD (high-throughput full polarimetric imaging), catboss (RFI flagging suite), and Foresight (sky model estimation). Experience with CASA, Python, C++, and HPC workflows.

Observational Facilities

Principal Investigator on 250 hours of uGMRT observations, 93.5 hours on the Jansky VLA, and 64 ks of XMM-Newton time. Co-investigator on additional observing proposals.
